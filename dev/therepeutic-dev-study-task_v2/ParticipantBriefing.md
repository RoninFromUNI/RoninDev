# TaskFlow — participant brief

hey, thanks for taking part in this study. here's everything you need to know before we start.

---

## what's this about

we're looking at how developers naturally approach debugging. no tricks, no hidden scoring, no right or wrong way to do it. we're just interested in your process.

you'll be working through a small Java project called **TaskFlow** — a task management app with some bugs baked into it. your job is to find and fix them.

---

## setup

1. open the **therepeutic-dev-study-task** project in IntelliJ IDEA
2. make sure it builds cleanly (it should, it's a plain Maven project with zero dependencies)
3. you should see a plugin panel called **Therapeutic Dev** in your tool window — just leave it running in the background, you don't need to interact with it
4. run `Main.java` and read the console output — that's your starting point

---

## what to do

- work through the bugs you find in whatever order feels natural
- fix them one at a time, re-run Main after each fix to see if the output changes
- there's no time limit but most people finish in about 60-90 minutes

---

## timestamp recording

we need you to jot down times as you go. nothing fancy, just:

use clock time (like 14:23) not a stopwatch. a sticky note, notepad, your phone — whatever works for you.

---

## ground rules

- use IntelliJ however you normally would — debugger, search, refactoring tools, whatever
- google and stack overflow are fine, this isn't an exam
- try not to use AI coding assistants (copilot, chatgpt, etc.) for this session since we're looking at your natural debugging patterns
- if you're genuinely stuck for more than 15 minutes on one bug, move on and come back to it
